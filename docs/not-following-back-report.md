# Not-Following-Back Report

The not-following-back report compares your Instagram following list against your Instagram follower list. It helps identify accounts you follow that do not appear in your follower export.

## What The Report Means

If an account appears in your following list but not in your follower list, the report may label it as not following back.

This is a comparison of exported lists. It is not official Instagram analytics and it does not access private platform data.

## How To Generate The Report

1. Download your Instagram data export.
2. Make sure the export includes both followers and following.
3. Open the export in Instagram Detailed List Viewer.
4. Load the relationship summary.
5. Open the not-following-back view.
6. Review the accounts before taking action.

## Why Results Can Differ From Instagram

Results may differ from what you see in the Instagram app when:

- Your export is old.
- An account changed its username.
- An account was deleted, disabled, blocked, or restricted.
- Instagram changed the export format.
- One of the required files is missing from the export.
- A relationship changed after you downloaded the export.

For current decisions, download a fresh export.

## Can It Show Who Unfollowed Me?

A single export usually shows current lists. It may not show a full history of who unfollowed you. To identify changes over time, save two or more exports and compare them by date.

For example:

- Export from January 1
- Export from February 1
- Compare follower lists to see which accounts were added or removed between the two snapshots

## Privacy Note

Only analyze exports you are allowed to process. Do not upload or inspect someone else's private data without permission.
